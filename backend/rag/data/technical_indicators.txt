# Technical Indicators Guide for Trading

## Overview
Technical indicators are mathematical calculations based on historical price data that help traders identify trends, momentum, and potential entry/exit points.

## Trend Indicators

### Moving Averages (MA)
**Simple Moving Average (SMA)**
- Calculates average price over specified period
- Smooths price action to identify trend direction
- Common periods: 20, 50, 200 days
- Use crossovers for signals: Golden cross (50 above 200) = bullish, Death cross (50 below 200) = bearish

**Exponential Moving Average (EMA)**
- Gives more weight to recent prices
- More responsive to price changes than SMA
- Popular periods: 12, 26 for MACD calculation
- EMA crossovers provide earlier signals than SMA

### Moving Average Convergence Divergence (MACD)
- Consists of MACD line, signal line, and histogram
- MACD line = 12-day EMA minus 26-day EMA
- Signal line = 9-day EMA of MACD line
- Histogram = MACD line minus signal line
- Bullish signal: MACD crosses above signal line
- Bearish signal: MACD crosses below signal line
- Divergence between price and MACD indicates potential reversal

### Average Directional Index (ADX)
- Measures trend strength regardless of direction
- Values above 25 indicate strong trend
- Values below 20 indicate weak trend or ranging market
- Use with +DI and -DI for direction signals
- Rising ADX = strengthening trend

## Momentum Indicators

### Relative Strength Index (RSI)
- Measures speed and change of price movements
- Range: 0 to 100
- Overbought: RSI > 70, Oversold: RSI < 30
- Divergence between RSI and price signals potential reversal
- RSI failure swings provide early reversal signals
- Best for ranging markets, less reliable in strong trends

### Stochastic Oscillator
- Compares closing price to price range over period
- Two lines: %K (fast) and %D (slow)
- Range: 0 to 100
- Overbought: > 80, Oversold: < 20
- Bullish signal: %K crosses above %D in oversold area
- Bearish signal: %K crosses below %D in overbought area

### Rate of Change (ROC)
- Measures percentage change in price over time
- Positive ROC = upward momentum
- Negative ROC = downward momentum
- Zero line crossover signals momentum shift
- Use with other indicators for confirmation

## Volatility Indicators

### Bollinger Bands
- Consists of middle band (SMA), upper band, and lower band
- Upper band = SMA + (2 × standard deviation)
- Lower band = SMA - (2 × standard deviation)
- Bands widen during high volatility, narrow during low volatility
- Price touching upper band = overbought condition
- Price touching lower band = oversold condition
- Squeeze (narrow bands) often precedes big price moves

### Average True Range (ATR)
- Measures market volatility
- Calculates average range over 14 periods
- Higher ATR = higher volatility
- Use for setting stop losses and position sizing
- ATR doesn't indicate direction, only volatility

### VIX (Volatility Index)
- Measures market's expectation of 30-day volatility
- Often called "fear index"
- High VIX = high fear, potential market bottom
- Low VIX = complacency, potential market top
- Use for market timing and risk management

## Volume Indicators

### On-Balance Volume (OBV)
- Cumulative volume indicator
- Adds volume on up days, subtracts on down days
- OBV trend should confirm price trend
- Divergence signals potential reversal
- Leading indicator that moves before price

### Volume Profile
- Shows volume traded at specific price levels
- Identifies support and resistance levels
- High volume nodes = strong support/resistance
- Low volume nodes = potential breakout areas
- Point of Control (POC) = highest volume price level

### Money Flow Index (MFI)
- RSI weighted by volume
- Range: 0 to 100
- Overbought: > 80, Oversold: < 20
- Combines price and volume analysis
- More reliable than RSI for confirming trends

## Combination Strategies

### Trend and Momentum Combo
- Use moving averages for trend direction
- Add RSI for entry timing in direction of trend
- Example: Buy when price above 200 SMA and RSI crosses above 30

### Volatility Breakout Strategy
- Use Bollinger Bands for volatility measurement
- Trade breakouts from Bollinger Band squeeze
- Confirm with volume increase
- Set stop loss at opposite band

### Divergence Trading
- Identify divergence between price and oscillator (RSI, MACD)
- Regular divergence: Price makes new high, indicator makes lower high (bearish)
- Hidden divergence: Price makes lower high, indicator makes higher high (bullish)
- Wait for price confirmation before entering

## Best Practices

1. **Use Multiple Indicators**: Combine 2-3 non-correlated indicators
2. **Confirm Signals**: Wait for multiple confirmations before entering
3. **Consider Timeframe**: Use longer timeframes for trend, shorter for entries
4. **Avoid Over-Optimization**: Don't use too many indicators
5. **Backtest Strategies**: Test indicator combinations historically
6. **Adjust for Market Conditions**: Different indicators work in different markets
7. **Use with Price Action**: Indicators should confirm, not replace, price analysis

## Common Mistakes

1. **Indicator Lag**: All indicators lag price, don't rely solely on them
2. **Over-Analysis**: Using too many indicators creates paralysis
3. **Ignoring Context**: Same signal means different things in different market conditions
4. **False Signals**: No indicator is 100% accurate, expect false signals
5. **Curve Fitting**: Optimizing parameters for past performance doesn't guarantee future results

Remember: Technical indicators are tools, not crystal balls. Use them to understand market psychology and probabilities, not to predict exact price movements.
